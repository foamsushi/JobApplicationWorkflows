### **Rule Set 1: The "Blacklisted" Vocabulary**

To ensure text does not sound like AI, authors must perform a "search and destroy" mission for the following high-frequency AI markers. These words should be replaced with simpler, more specific alternatives.

#### **1.1 The "Big Three": Delve, Foster, Tapestry**

These three words are the "flagships" of AI writing. Their presence in a text, especially in close proximity, significantly increases the likelihood of a false positive from AI detection tools and human readers alike.

* **DELVE (esp. "Delve into")**  
  * *The AI Habit:* AI uses "delve into" as a universal transition to introduce a topic (e.g., "Let's delve into the intricacies of..."). It is a "throat-clearing" mechanism.4  
  * *The Forensic Data:* Analysis of biomedical abstracts showed a massive spike in the word "delve" post-2023.5  
  * *The Human Rule:* **Never use "delve" to introduce a topic.** If you mean "investigate," "analyze," "study," or "explore," use those words. Better yet, cut the introduction entirely and start with the facts.  
  * *Correction Example:*  
    * *AI:* "In this article, we will delve into the history of..."  
    * *Human:* "The history of..."  
* **FOSTER**  
  * *The AI Habit:* AI loves to "foster" abstract concepts: fostering innovation, fostering community, fostering a culture of inclusivity.6 It is a safe verb that implies positive action without requiring a specific description of *how* that action is taken.  
  * *The Forensic Data:* Users report that even when explicitly prompted with negative constraints ("Do not use the word foster"), models like ChatGPT will still produce it, indicating a deep-seated weight in the neural network.6  
  * *The Human Rule:* **Replace "foster" with the specific mechanism of action.** Did you "build," "fund," "organize," "encourage," or "create"? Use the verb that describes the physical reality.  
    * *AI:* " The program aims to foster innovation."  
    * *Human:* "The program funds experimental research."  
* **TAPESTRY (and "Landscape", "Realm")**  
  * *The AI Habit:* AI uses "tapestry" to describe complexity (e.g., "a rich tapestry of culture"). It uses "landscape" to describe a field (e.g., "the digital landscape"). It uses "realm" to describe a domain (e.g., "the realm of biology").  
  * *The Forensic Data:* These words act as "semantic buckets"—they are broad containers that allow the AI to sound profound while saying nothing specific.6  
  * *The Human Rule:* **Ban the metaphor of the loom.** Unless you are writing about actual textiles, do not use "tapestry." Replace "landscape" with "market," "industry," or "environment." Replace "realm" with "field" or "area."

#### **1.2 The "Corporate Inflater" Lexicon**

Beyond the Big Three, there is a tier of "puffery" words that Wikipedia editors describe as "peacocking".1 These words inflate the importance of the subject without evidence.

**Table 2: The "Corporate Inflater" Substitution Protocol**

| AI Marker | Why AI Uses It | The Human Replacement (Rule) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **"Testament to"** | Passive construction that links cause and effect grandiosely (e.g., "It is a testament to his skill"). 4 | **Show, Don't Tell.** "His skill is proven by..." or simply state the achievement. |
| **"Underscores"** | A favorite transition verb to imply significance (e.g., "This underscores the importance of..."). | **"Shows," "Demonstrates," "Proves,"** or delete the sentence entirely. |
| **"Crucial / Pivotal / Paramount"** | Hyperbolic adjectives used to describe mundane details. | **"Important," "Key,"** or let the facts establish importance. |
| **"Vibrant / Bustling"** | Default descriptors for cities, markets, or communities. 8 | **Specific Sensory Details.** "Noisy," "Crowded," "Neon-lit," "Growing." |
| **"Seamless / Seamlessly"** | Overused adverb for integration or technology. 7 | **"Easy," "Smooth," "Automatic,"** or describe the lack of friction. |
| **"Game-changer / Revolutionary"** | Marketing cliché that AI treats as fact. 1 | **Delete.** Describe the specific change in metrics or behavior. |
| **"Meticulous / Meticulously"** | Adverb used to pad descriptions of work/research. 6 | **"Careful," "Detailed,"** or describe the methodology. |
| **"Navigating the complexities of"** | A generic phrase used to describe dealing with difficulty. 4 | **"Solving," "Managing," "Handling,"** or "Working through." |
